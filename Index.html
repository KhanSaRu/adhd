Attention-Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder

ADHD is a very common mental illness. It is growing more in prevelance as more information is being learned about it. The stigma around ADHD is high. However as more of the general population is being educated about this issue, more people are finally becoming more open-minded. One very common misconception is that boys are more likely to get diagnosed with ADHD then girls. Society thinks that individuals with ADHD are incapable of functioning as normal members of society. As you explore through the rest of the website you will be able to learn more about ADHD, what it is, who it effects and hopefully leave behind any outdated ideas you might've had before finding this site.


A little bit of history!!

Until the late 1980s, the term for this mental illness was ADD (Attention Deficit Disorder), with the term hyperactive added to it if that side of the illness was present or not added if those symptoms were absent. But this classification system changes in the early 1990s. Starting then, children were being diagnosed with one of the three types of ADHD. The first one being inattentive type, the second is hyperactie/impulsive type and the last is combined type for the children who show a comination of the other two symptoms.
