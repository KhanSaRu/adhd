Here are some statistics around the ADHD topic. These statistics are from Canada as well as worldwide.

  • Boys are more likely to be diagnosed with ADHD than girls

  • 6 out of 10 children with ADHD are reported to have other mental, emotional or behavioural disorders
    • Behaviour or conduct problems
    • Anxiety
    • Depression
    • Autism spectrum disorder
    • Tourette syndrome


  • 3 out of 4 children were receiving treatment for ADHD

  • High school graduates with ADHD earn an average of 17% less annual income than those without ADHD

  • Childhood ADHD is associated with an increased risk of death before the age of 46

  • Approximately 129 million children and adolescents worldwide between the ages of 5 to 19 years old have ADHD

  • More than 366 million adults worldwide have ADHD as of 2020

  • 1 in 21 Canadians have ADHD

  • ADHD is classified as a neurodevelopmental disorder. It has been documented for more than 200 years

  • Eighty percent of children diagnosed with ADHD continue to qualify for a diagnosis in adolescence and at least 65% continue to be impaired by symptoms in adulthood

  • All forms of attention regulation are impaired in ADHD.
    • Overfocus and difficulty breaking their focus
    • Under focusing
